Honest Verdict

For Borrowers — Still cheaper than industry

  • 2 months: Borrower pays R1,308 vs R1,361 industry max. 3.9% cheaper.
  • 6 months: Borrower pays R1,599 vs R1,707 industry max. 6.3% cheaper.
  • Longer terms create bigger savings because the interest rate gap (30% vs 60%) has more months to compound.
  • No credit life, transparent fees, and a lower initiation (R150 vs R165).

For the Business — Now viable

  • Revenue per R1k/2mo: R188 (was R15–152). You earn 78% of industry revenue.
  • Revenue per R1k/6mo: R239 (was R83–155). You earn 69% of industry revenue.
  • Default buffer: 5.3 loans to cover a 2-month default, 4.2 for 6-month. Industry is 4.2 and 2.9 respectively. Your buffer is slightly thinner but manageable with good credit checks.
  • Initiation on every loan is the anchor — it guarantees R150 per R1,000 regardless of term or whether the borrower is new or returning.
  • Returning borrowers no longer earn you only R15 per R1,000. Now it's R185–233 depending on term.
My take: This model hits the sweet spot. Borrowers still save 4–7% vs industry, but you now earn real revenue. The old model was great for borrowers but unsustainable for you (R15 per R1k returning loan). The new model makes the interest rate do real work while keeping you comfortably below NCA maximums. The 30% p.a. rate produces exactly half the interest of the industry-standard 60%, which is a great marketing message: "Pay half the interest of the big lenders."
